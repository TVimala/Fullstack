Hyderabad

Hyderabad was founded in 1591 by Mohammed Quli Qutub Shah, the fifth sultan of the Qutb Shahi dynasty. The city has a rich cultural and historical tradition that spans over 400 years. Hyderabad is known for its monuments, including the Charminar and the Golconda fort. The city is also home to distinctive mosques, Hindu temple gods, churches, and Gurudwaras. Hyderabad is a multilingual city, with four languages spoken: Hindi, Urdu, Telugu, and English. English is the language of business and administration.
